Icarus: Exploring the Russian Doping Scandal

Oscar Award Winner | Bryan Fogel | 1h 21m

WHAT'S IT ABOUT?

Bryan Fogel, the director and an amateur bike racer, planned to investigate the use of performance-enhancing drugs by taking them himself and then trying to evade detection. The idea, a sort of “Super Size Me” with steroids instead of Big Macs, brought him into contact with Dr. Grigory Rodchenkov, who led Russia’s antidoping laboratory for Olympic athletes. Dr. Grigory turns into a whistleblower and works with Bryan to expose the sponsored cheating at the 2014 Sochi Winter Olymic Games.

MY THOUGHTS

Gripping, thought provoking and excellent direction and acting. All the risks taken by Fogel make it hard to believe that he was alone till the end. The storytelling of learning, understanding and acceptance as the three stages plus the queit demonstration of true friendship to the end were other additional assets Not just sports, there is probably a thin line between anything and politics. Sometimes the system is so broken from inside, and so dirty from within that even truth fails, how ever big or may be. If you want insight into cheating in sports and the complicated reasons why it happens over and over, Icarus will open your eyes as never before. It is essential viewing for anyone interested in athletics or competitive sports of any type.
